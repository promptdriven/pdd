Frame at t=52s: Same split screen. The wooden chair on the LEFT now has a visible golden/warm glowing aura radiating outward from it. The subtitle text reads: "In crafting, value lives in the object..." The mold on the RIGHT does not yet have a visible aura at this moment.

Frame at t=54s: LEFT side: The chair's golden aura is now very prominent -- a bright warm glow surrounding the entire chair object. RIGHT side: The mold diagram and parts remain without an obvious aura effect on the mold specifically. The subtitle continues: "In crafting, value lives in the object..."

## Verdict: MODERATE

## Severity: MODERATE

## Notes
- The split-screen layout matches the script well: wooden chair on LEFT, injection mold on RIGHT.
- The glowing aura on the chair (LEFT) is clearly visible and matches the script's intent ("aura surrounds the wooden chair -- the object itself").
- ISSUE: The aura on the RIGHT side should surround the MOLD (not the plastic parts), but the mold does not appear to have a prominent glowing aura in the frames captured. The visual emphasis on the RIGHT side's mold-as-value is weaker than the LEFT side's chair-as-value.
- The script specifically calls for a craftsman "hand-carving" but the LEFT shows a simplified illustrated chair without a craftsman figure. This is acceptable for the motion-graphics style.
- The subtitle text helps convey the message but the visual asymmetry (strong aura left, weak/absent aura right) partially undermines the contrast the script intends.
