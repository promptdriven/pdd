## Script Visual
> "The sock metaphor returns one final time. A holey sock. A person considers it for a moment, then discards it and grabs a fresh one."

## Frame-by-Frame Analysis

### t2.7s
A single sock is displayed against a dark brown/warm background. The sock is beige/tan colored, L-shaped, and clearly has a dark hole (elliptical shape) visible in the foot area. No person or figure is visible. The sock is centered in the upper portion of the frame.

### t5.0s
Same sock with hole, same position. A small "$0.50" price tag has appeared in the upper right corner. The sock remains static. Still no person/figure visible.

### t7.3s
The sock has now been rotated/tilted diagonally (as if being tossed or discarded). Small dots/particles appear near the hole, suggesting motion or discard action. The sock is in the left-center of the frame, appearing to tumble. No fresh sock replacement is visible in this frame.

## Compliance Assessment

| Criterion | Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Sock metaphor returns | PASS | Sock is shown, consistent with earlier metaphor |
| Holey sock | PASS | Clear dark hole visible in the foot area |
| Person considers it | PARTIAL | No visible person/figure; the "$0.50" price tag implies cost consideration rather than a character considering |
| Discards sock | PASS | The tilted/tumbling sock at t7.3s with particles suggests discarding |
| Grabs fresh one | FAIL | No replacement/fresh sock is shown in any frame within this segment |

## Overall Verdict: PARTIAL PASS

The holey sock is well-rendered and the discard action is implied through the tumbling animation. However, two elements are missing: (1) there is no visible person/figure "considering" the sock -- the consideration is implied abstractly through the price tag rather than through a character, and (2) no fresh replacement sock appears. The "grabs a fresh one" action is entirely absent, which weakens the metaphor's payoff about replacing rather than patching.

## Recommendations
- Add a brief appearance of a new pristine sock (without holes) entering from the side after the old one is discarded
- Consider adding a simple silhouette or stick figure to represent the "person" who considers and discards
